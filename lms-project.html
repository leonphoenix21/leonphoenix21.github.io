Learning Management System

Xashy Tech University (XTU) is owned by Xashy Inc., an American-based software consulting company. Their mission is to expand access to education in the tech industry. This LMS was built to streamline administrative processes, provide robust learning tools, and support XTU’s goal of empowering students around the globe.

Client

Xashy Tech University: An American-based university focused on tech education and innovation.

Problem: Manual processes for course enrollment, financial transactions, and assessment tracking led to inefficiencies and errors.

Goal: Provide a holistic LMS to streamline registration, improve data accuracy, and enhance user experience for students and faculty.

Objectives

  • 3. Enhance engagement through interactive, multimedia-rich learning experiences.
  • 4. Track and monitor learner progress and performance in real-time.
  • 5. Automate administrative tasks like enrollment, grading, and attendance tracking.
  • 6. Foster communication and collaboration among students, instructors, and administrators.
  • 7. Scale effortlessly to accommodate more users, courses, and programs.
  • 8. Ensure data security, privacy, and compliance with regulations.
  • 9. Reduce reliance on physical resources, optimizing cost and efficiency.
  • 10. Provide feedback and analytics to evaluate and improve learning outcomes.

Key Features
  • Role-Based Access Control: Admins, Teachers, and Students have distinct privileges.
  • Profile Management: Users can update personal details like contact info and academic data.
  • Course Registration: Students can browse and enroll in courses.
  • Course Materials: Teachers upload syllabi, lecture notes, and assignments; students can view them via their dashboards.
  • Course Scheduling: Admins can set up and manage schedules for all courses.
  • Assessment Module: Includes quizzes and exams with multiple-choice or structured questions.
  • Automatic Grading: Instant feedback for auto-graded assignments.
  • Gradebook: Teachers manage grades; students access them securely.
  • Admin Dashboard: Oversee users, courses, payments, and global settings.
  • Teacher Dashboard: Manage materials, track student performance.
  • Student Dashboard: View courses, grades, and assessment results.
  • Search and Access: Students find books, journals, and other materials.
  • Request System: Students can request materials not currently available.
  • Fee Tracking: Students see tuition fees and account balances.
  • Secure Payments: Integrated gateways for online transactions.
  • Transaction History: Detailed payment logs for transparency.
  • Zoom Integration: Teachers can schedule and host live classes.
  • Interactive Features: Screen sharing, chat, and Q&A for robust learning sessions.
  • Live Chat Integration: Powered by Tawk.to for real-time assistance.
  • Knowledge Base: FAQs and resources for self-service support.
  • Event Management: Centralized calendar for events and clubs.
  • Club Directory: Students can discover and join organizations.
  • Event Registration: Streamlined sign-up process for campus events.
  • Career Resources: Job postings, internships, and more.
  • Notifications: Real-time updates on new opportunities.
  • Email and SMS Alerts: Automated reminders and updates.
  • In-System Messaging: Enables communication between students, teachers, and admins.
  • Data Insights: Generate reports on registrations, attendance, and financial transactions.
  • Export Options: PDF or Excel formats for administrators.
Challenges & Solutions
  • Issue: Initially, the project manager and I reviewed and approved designs before development. However, some developed pages and features did not align with the client’s expectations, leading to rework and delays.
  • Resolution:
    • Introduced a process where the client previewed all pages and features planned for development the following week.
    • Ensured the client’s feedback was incorporated early in the design stage, reducing the risk of misalignment.
    • Improved communication channels among design, development, and client teams to maintain consistent alignment.
  • Issue: Initially deployed on Heroku, the platform inconsistencies caused discrepancies between the local environment and the live site, leading to unpredictable behavior and debugging difficulties.
  • Resolution:
    • Switched the deployment platform from Heroku to AWS for better control and reliability.
    • Leveraged Docker to containerize the application, ensuring consistent behavior across local and production environments.
    • Set up GitHub CI/CD pipelines to automate builds, tests, and deployments, streamlining the release process.
  • Issue: At the 1.5-month mark, it became evident that the workload and complexity of the frontend were higher than anticipated, causing delays that threatened the project timeline.
  • Resolution:
    • Collaborated with the product owner to assess the project scope and identify bottlenecks.
    • Reached out to professional contacts and onboarded one contract frontend engineer within a week.
    • Added a second contract engineer a month later to accelerate development and meet the deadline.
